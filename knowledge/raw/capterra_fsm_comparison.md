## Essential field service management software buying information

[Field service management (FSM) software](https://www.capterra.com/field-service-management-software/) enables organizations to manage resources and communicate with field employees. Field service management systems enhance productivity by allowing constant contact between the dispatch office and offsite personnel. FSM software improves the customer experience by giving field personnel access to service histories, mapping services, scheduling tools and the organization's knowledge base resources.

Field service management software is utilized by various industries that dispatch employees or technicians to provide services at customer locations, including construction firms, HVAC, facilities management, and security companies. The software aids route optimization, allowing technicians to minimize travel time and reduce overall fuel costs.

Most FSM systems on the market are priced on a “per month” subscription basis. Costs for popular platforms typically cost $100 per month for a basic system. Premium or more advanced systems commonly cost around $450. As the cost increases, more functionality and more users are added.\[ [1](https://www.capterra.com/field-service-management-software/#sources)\]

First-time field service management software buyers should consider several key factors when purchasing the tool, including ease of use and necessary integrations. Ensure you have a list of questions to help you evaluate software based on your needs, such as:

- How does the software support mobile access for field technicians?

- Can it handle scheduling, dispatching, routing, and tracking of field technicians?

- How easy is it to search and retrieve service history records?

- Can the tool generate reports on key metrics like service response times, resolution rates, or recurring issues?

- Can users view calendars by day, week, month, or other customizable timeframes?

## What is field service management software?

[Field service management software](https://www.capterra.com/field-service-management-software/), also known as field service software, helps businesses manage and schedule field operations, such as assigning tasks to field workers or managing inventory and parts. Field service management software assists with tracking on-site job progress and facilitating communication and collaboration between field technicians, managers, and customers.

Capterra covers 1,047 field service management products and published 6,311 reviews from users of this software in the past year.\[ [3](https://www.capterra.com/field-service-management-software/#sources)\]

## Field service management software pricing

Most FSM systems on the market are priced on a “per month” subscription basis. Costs for popular platforms commonly cost $100 per month for a basic system all the way up to typically $450 per month for a more advanced and customized field service management software suite.

- Entry-level field service management tools include the core functionality of billing and invoicing, service history and schedule management.

- A premium or advanced product that’s priced higher typically includes additional features like unlimited users and advanced reporting.


More than half (51%) of the field service management software buyers Capterra advisors spoke to in the last year are budgeting $50 to $100 per user per month.\[ [4](https://www.capterra.com/field-service-management-software/#sources)\] And based on 111 products on Capterra, 75% start at less than $50 per month.

First-time buyers should be aware that:

- Perpetual license cost, customization, installation and integration with existing systems are upfront costs.

- System maintenance (e.g., updates, patches and upgrades) and support services are common recurring costs.


Many field service management platforms offer [free versions](https://www.capterra.com/field-service-management-software/s/free/) and free trials to allow buyers to get an initial feel for their features. Software buyers may want to explore the user interface and functionalities to better understand how the platform works and see if it aligns with their needs.

## Field service management software features rated by users

All field service management tools include dispatch management, mobile access, scheduling and work order management. Most share a variety of common features such as billing and invoicing, electronic signature, GPS and inventory management.

### Core field service management software features

- **Mobile access:** Access crucial information, perform various tasks and facilitate on-the-go communication between the field team and office. 89% rate this feature as critical or highly important.

- **Scheduling:** Plan the availability of resources and assign specific time slots for each task. 87% rate this feature as critical or highly important.

- **Work order management:** Schedule tasks, receive real-time updates on the status of work orders and facilitate communication between dispatchers, technicians, and customers. 78% rate this feature as critical or highly important.

- **Dispatch management:** Schedule and assign service appointments to field technicians based on factors such as technician availability, skills, location, and urgency of the job. 77% rate this feature as critical or highly important.


### Common field service management software features

- **Service history:** Track the job performed, resources used, technicians involved, and service type for every customer. 85% rate this feature as critical or highly important.

- **Billing and invoicing:** Automate the invoicing process and aid in reducing delays in billing customers for services rendered. 75% rate this feature as critical or highly important.

- **Contact database:** Consolidate all customer information into a single, centralized location, making it easy for users to access and update customer records as needed.

- **Quotes/estimates:** Provides accurate material and labor costs, and maintains a history of all quotes provided, allowing for easy reference and follow-up.

## Common challenges of field service management software identified by users

When buying new platforms, 38% of software purchasers disclose that addressing security concerns is a top challenge, while another 37% mention issues with finding software compatible with existing systems.\[ [5](https://www.capterra.com/field-service-management-software/#sources)\]

Regarding the implementation of field service management software, user reviews most frequently cited issue resolution, cancellation and refunds, and customer payment management as the tool's most common limitations.

When researching field service management tools, businesses must clearly specify their expectations of how certain features (e.g., those related to cancellations or customer payment methods) function. Implementing notifications that trigger automatically when a cancellation or refund request is received is key for efficient customer service and the organization’s reputation.

## What is field service management software used for?

Analysis of our extensive field service management software reviews highlights the most common use cases:

- **Technician task automation:** The tool automates scheduling and dispatching, and generates work orders, as well as enabling technicians to automate repetitive tasks and standardize service procedures using digital checklists and workflows.

- **Optimized customer service:** FSM software enables dispatchers to quickly assign received requests to field technicians, leading to faster response times and shorter wait times for customers, improving overall customer satisfaction.

- **Supports tracking:** FSM tools allow tracking capabilities, such as GPS and geofencing, to provide real-time location tracking of the field technicians. This helps dispatchers monitor each technician's exact location, optimize routing, and reduce travel time.

[Field service management (FSM) software](https://www.capterra.com/field-service-management-software/) enables organizations to manage resources, communicate with field employees, and enhances productivity by allowing constant contact between the dispatch office and offsite personnel.

This software is utilized by various industries that dispatch employees or technicians to provide services at customer locations, including construction firms, HVAC, facilities management, and security companies.

A typical entry-level pricing plan could run around $100 per month. Premium and advanced system subscriptions commonly cost about $450 or more per month.

When acquiring this type of tool, in addition to evaluating price and integration capabilities with other tools, users should also ask vendors the following questions:

_How does the software support mobile access for field technicians?_

_Can it handle scheduling, dispatching, routing, and tracking of field technicians?_

_How easy is it to search and retrieve service history records?_

_Can users view calendars by day, week, month, or other customizable timeframes?_
