# Simpro Group — Company Overview

## What Simpro Does

Simpro Group builds cloud-based field service management (FSM) software for trade and service contractors. Its flagship product, simPRO, helps businesses manage the full job lifecycle: quoting, scheduling, dispatching, job tracking, invoicing, and reporting — all in one platform. The company targets small-to-midsize trade businesses in electrical, plumbing, HVAC, fire protection, security, and solar industries.

## Scale and Market Position

- **Customers:** 22,000+ businesses across Australia, New Zealand, the United States, and the United Kingdom
- **Users:** 300,000+ field and office workers on the platform
- **Founded:** 2002 in Brisbane, Australia
- **Headquarters:** Brisbane, Australia; with offices in Denver (US), London (UK), and Auckland (NZ)
- **Ownership:** Private; Vista Equity Partners took a majority stake in 2021

Simpro positions itself as the enterprise-grade option in FSM — more feature-rich and scalable than lighter tools like Jobber or HouseCallPro, but less expensive than enterprise platforms like Salesforce Field Service or ServiceMax. It competes most directly with ServiceTitan in the US mid-market.

## Products

| Product | Target Customer | Core Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| simPRO | Trade contractors (5–500 employees) | End-to-end job and field service management |
| BuildXact | Residential builders and remodelers | Estimating, scheduling, project management |
| Clockshark | Field service and construction | Time tracking and crew scheduling |

BuildXact and Clockshark were acquired to expand the product portfolio beyond simPRO's core trade contractor market.

## Revenue Model

Simpro operates a SaaS subscription model. Pricing is not publicly listed; it is quoted per-business based on the number of users, modules required, and contract length. Typical contract values for mid-market customers (50–200 users) range from $1,000–$5,000/month. Implementation and onboarding fees apply for enterprise customers.

## Growth Strategy

- **Geographic expansion:** Strong ANZ base; actively growing US and UK presence
- **Product expansion:** Acquisitions of BuildXact and Clockshark extend TAM beyond core FSM
- **Upmarket movement:** Investing in enterprise sales and compliance features for larger contractors
- **Partner ecosystem:** Building integrations with accounting software (Xero, QuickBooks, MYOB), ERP systems, and IoT platforms

## Key Metrics a RevOps Analyst Would Track

- **ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue):** Primary growth indicator
- **NRR (Net Revenue Retention):** Measures expansion within existing accounts
- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost):** Cost to acquire a new customer
- **LTV (Lifetime Value):** Projected revenue from a customer over the relationship
- **Churn Rate:** Monthly/annual customer cancellation rate
- **Pipeline Conversion Rate:** % of deals progressing from stage to stage
- **Average Deal Size:** Average ARR per new customer
- **Sales Cycle Length:** Days from first contact to closed-won
